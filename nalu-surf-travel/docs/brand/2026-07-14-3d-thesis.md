# Birdie Surf Travel — Why 3D (and what only 3D can do)

**For:** owner + implementing engineer · **Date:** 2026-07-14
**Grounding:** `src/scene/*` as shipped 2026-07-13 (fixed camera z=3.2→2.4, globe rotates under a screen-fixed heli, canvas-painted equirect texture, terrain relief ≤0.032R, 12 destinations).
**Constraints respected throughout:** R3F primitives/procedural only, dpr [1,2], no post-processing, `prefers-reduced-motion` degrades everything.

---

## 1. The thesis — why this must be a planet, not a map

1. **The Pacific is the product, and every 2D map tears it in half.** 7 of the 12 destinations are Pacific (Oahu −158°, Teahupo'o −149°, Snapper 153°, Cloudbreak 177°, Raglan 175°, Nosara, Puerto Escondido). On any flat projection the dateline cut lands exactly on this cluster — Cloudbreak and Raglan sit at the torn edge, and "fly from Hawaii to Fiji" means teleporting across the page. On the sphere the Pacific is one continuous ocean and flying west from Oahu simply arrives. A surf-travel site whose core geography breaks the projection is the rare product where the globe is *correct*, not decorative.
2. **Curvature is honest fog-of-war.** At z=3.2 the camera sees a ~72° cap; the far hemisphere is genuinely occluded by the planet's body. Discovery-through-travel is enforced by geometry, not by an arbitrary mask — and because markers protrude (0.085R poles/boards), tips crest the horizon *before* their bases, the ships-masts-over-the-curve effect. No 2D map hides anything without visible artifice.
3. **One light vector buys a world of derived meaning.** A single directional light on a sphere yields a terminator, a golden-hour band, limb shading, and relief shadows — all physically coherent, all free. On a flat map each is a separately painted overlay that breaks when scrolled. The existing 3-step toon gradient even makes the night side a dusky ~38% step, not black — brand-safe darkness falls out of the shading model.
4. **Only a 3D camera can travel from planet to place without a cut.** 2D zoom is scaling; 3D zoom is *perspective change* — horizon forms, foreshortening appears, a map pin becomes a location you stand at. That continuous world→destination move is the medium's signature shot and maps 1:1 onto the booking funnel (world → spot → book).
5. **Honesty clause: the current build barely uses any of this.** `stepFlight` is an axis-aligned lat/lng integrator (a 2D scroll with wraparound), the camera is bolted to +Z, lighting is static, swell lines are baked paint. Today's scene is a beautiful 2D experience *rendered* in 3D — points 1–2 are working implicitly, 3–4 not at all. The proposals below convert the renderer into a medium.

Marginal-in-3D (say it plainly): flag billboards, marker bobbing, the peel-loop crescent as seen top-down, and the art-direction §1 swell-drift texture crossfade would all work identically on a 2D map. They're charm, not argument.

## 2. 3D-only opportunities

Each: what · why 2D can't · what it unlocks (player / funnel+SEO) · feasibility + how.

**A. Descend-from-orbit: the landing becomes a place — FLAGSHIP, MEDIUM**
On `landed`, the camera leaves the +Z axis: dolly in and tilt ~55° off-normal so the globe limb becomes a *horizon* under the sky gradient, framing the existing vignette (board pin, peeling wave, landmark, flag, parked heli) as a low-angle diorama. Take-off reverses it. 2D-crux: a flat map can only cut or scale; it can never turn a pin into a standing-there view in one continuous move. Unlocks: the info card now sits beside a postcard *place* → conversion framing; 12 distinct low-angle postcards → 12 per-spot OG images + Astro-generated `/spots/{id}` pages deep-linking into this framing (real SEO surface, not just wow). Redeems already-paid costs: terrain relief and §5c landmarks are barely legible top-down but read fully at a low angle. How: camera-only — extend `CameraZoom` into a position+lookAt spline (the "NEAR need not keep the whole sphere in frame" owner precedent in `GlobeScene.tsx` already licenses breaking whole-globe framing while landed); reduced-motion cuts to the framing with a 300ms fade. Genuine 3D unlock.

**B. Real-clock sun: live terminator + "dawn patrol is happening now" — EASY/MEDIUM**
Compute the subsolar point from actual UTC (solar declination + hour angle, ~10 lines), mount the key light *inside* the rig at `latLngToVec3(subsolarLat, subsolarLng, 5)` so day/night is geographically true while the globe rotates. The toon gradient renders a chunky stylized terminator — toy, not simulator. Surface it in product: destinations currently inside the golden band get a "dawn patrol now at Uluwatu — 6:14am" chip; night-side spots get 2–3 warm emissive dots (village lights, canvas texture layer 2). 2D-crux: a flat map's terminator is a painted sinusoid that lights nothing — no limb shading, no relief, no cloud glow. Unlocks: the site changes every hour → return visits, screenshot variance, an in-fiction urgency hook for booking ("it's firing there right now"). Cost: zero new geometry; the decorative `SunDisc` moves into the rig (and honestly sets). Reduced motion: static — it's positional, not animated. Genuine 3D unlock. Tension to resolve: the brief's "perpetual dawn patrol" — resolution is that the *terminator band* IS dawn patrol, permanently somewhere; clamp night to the dusky toon step so the mood never goes cold.

**C. Great-circle swell trains from real storm sources — MEDIUM**
Seasonal storm sources (Aleutian low → Oahu Nov–Feb; Roaring Forties → Fiji/Tahiti/Snapper Apr–Oct; N Atlantic → Ericeira/Hossegor Sep–Nov; S Atlantic → J-Bay Jun–Aug — the `bestWindow` data already encodes this) emit expanding swell rings that march across the ocean and arrive at the break. 2D-crux: swell really travels on great circles; a sphere small-circle at angular radius α is *exactly* a planar circle at distance cos α along the source axis with radius sin α — one torus per ring, position `S·cosα·1.002`, scale `sinα`, animate α. On a flat map the same circle is a distorted bulge no one reads. Unlocks: the agency's expertise made visible — the info card's "Nov–Feb" stops being a claim and becomes a storm you can watch feed Pipeline ("this wave was born 3,000 miles away"); unique explanatory copy for SEO. ~3 arc-limited toruses per active season, trivial budget; reduced motion: static arcs. Genuine 3D unlock — the *educational* one. (Live marine-API data: skip in v1; seasonal hardcode delivers 90% of the story with zero runtime dependency.)

**D. Horizon-crest discovery: make the occlusion a mechanic — EASY**
Amplify what the sphere already does: when an unvisited marker's tip crosses the limb (dot-product threshold against the camera axis), fire a one-shot sparkle billboard and a Nalu hint ("something's breaking over the horizon…"); add a "5 of 12 spots found" chip. 2D-crux: nothing is ever hidden on a flat map, so "found" is meaningless there. Unlocks: a collection loop → players sweep the whole planet → all 12 info cards get read (each is a booking entry point); longer sessions. How: per-marker `visible-last-frame` flag + pooled sparkle; `flyTo` quick-jump already preserves travel. Genuine, and nearly free.

**E. Intro establishing orbit: camera as narrator — EASY/MEDIUM**
Before handing over control, one scripted half-orbit of the rig (or camera) that makes 4–5 markers crest the horizon in rhythm — "here is your world, most of it still hidden" — ending on the existing hero close-up. 2D-crux: a map pan reveals everything at once; only a sphere gives a *reveal rhythm*. Unlocks: the wow beat lands in the first 10 seconds, where the competition judges form their impression. How: a rotation timeline on the existing rig refs; reduced motion: skip straight to hero pose. Genuine but modest — do it after A/B.

**F. Cloud-layer flythroughs + parallax altitude — EASY**
The heli orbits at ~1.26R; cloud puffs occupy a shell around 1.2R (tops ≈1.29R) — flythroughs already happen by accident. Make them deliberate: 2–3 more puffs on high-traffic routes, a 150ms cream veil on penetration, and let the existing altitude shadow sweep visibly over terrain bumps. 2D-crux: layer parallax and passing *through* things need depth. Unlocks: every flying screenshot gains depth cues; flight feels airborne rather than scrolled. Honest tag: real 3D, small payoff — a garnish, not an argument.

**G. Spherical flight: great-circle autopilot first, momentum second — MEDIUM**
`flyTo` currently lerps lat/lng, so quick-jumps slide along graticule paths; route it along the great circle instead (slerp the position vector) — Ericeira→Oahu visibly arcs toward the pole, a real aviation truth flat maps hide. Optionally later: heading-based integrator with momentum for manual flight. 2D-crux: great-circle motion is sphere-native. Honest verdict: genuine but low-visibility for casual players; the axis-aligned manual controls are *more* legible and should stay. Autopilot-only is the right cut.

**H. WebXR/AR "hold the planet" — REJECTED (gimmick here)**
Genuinely 3D-only, but: no iOS Safari WebXR, judges likely score on desktop, and it serves neither booking nor SEO. High cost, fragile, off-brief. Do not build.

## 3. Recommended flagship + runner-up

**Flagship: A — descend-from-orbit landing diorama.** It is the one feature a 2D map cannot approximate at any budget (perspective change + horizon formation, no cut); it lands exactly on the funnel's decisive beat (landed → read card → book); the brand brief already names the landing THE signature screenshot; and it's the highest-leverage build in the codebase — camera-only, riding existing touchdown coords, vignettes, `CameraZoom`, and the owner's own framing precedent, while retroactively justifying terrain relief and landmarks. Deliver with per-spot OG images + `/spots/{id}` pages and it is simultaneously the wow entry and the SEO entry.

**Runner-up: B — real-clock terminator.** Cheapest possible "this is a real planet" proof: one light moved inside the rig turns the site into a living object that's different at every visit, gives Nalu a truthful product line ("dawn at Snapper right now"), and photographs differently every hour — judges comparing notes will literally see different screenshots.

## 4. What to cut (3D cost without 3D payoff)

- **Swell-drift texture crossfade (§1 STRETCH, unbuilt):** two extra full-sphere transparent textures for a scrolling-paint effect that is 2D at heart. Never build; concept C supersedes it with real geometry for less memory.
- **Terrain relief — conditional:** from the fixed top-down camera the displacement is radial-toward-camera (invisible except limb silhouette) yet taxes every planted object with `heightAt` plumbing and forced the 96×64 sphere. If flagship A ships, it earns its keep at low angle; if A is cut, revert to 48×32 and delete the height plumbing.
- **`heightAt` calls on `BreakingWave`/open-water positions:** always 0 by construction — dead lookups, delete.
- **Flag billboarding in the diorama:** camera-facing flags read as stickers from a low angle; inside flagship framing, blend flags back to tangent orientation (the pole already is).
- **Landmark scale hacks (`LANDMARK_SCALE = 1.8`, `RENDER_OFFSET_SCALE = 3`):** compensations for the top-down view; re-tune (likely down) once A gives them a real viewing angle, rather than layering more compensations.
- **Second tail-rotor pinwheel + blur disc:** three meshes and a per-frame material write for a detail nobody can resolve at 0.09 scale from 2.4 units. Cut the disc at minimum.
