# Content Guidelines (from Gilad, 2026-07-09)

Guidelines for all user-facing content on Birdie Breaks. Re-read before writing or reviewing any copy, imagery, or CTA work (intro dialogue, info cards, booking calendar, side nav, README-facing copy, and the final polish pass).

1. **Focus on clarity** — Visitors should understand the business purpose within three seconds.
2. **Align content and visuals** — Headlines and images must communicate the same message.
3. **Use relevant imagery** — Choose images that clearly depict the business subject, avoiding generic visuals.
4. **Prioritize quality** — Use sharp, high-resolution images and avoid overly polished AI-generated content.
5. **Refine your text** — Keep titles and captions simple, avoid periods in titles, and use action-oriented verbs for CTA buttons.
6. **Stay unique** — Avoid generic "vibe-coded" results; seek inspiration from real websites and professional templates.

## Application notes for this project

- The approved art direction (toy low-poly 3D, spec §3) governs visuals; guidelines 3–4 apply to any 2D imagery/copy surrounding the game and to keeping the 3D art *specific* (surf pins, palm trees, the nene) rather than generic.
- Guideline 1: the intro speech bubble is the "three seconds" — Nalu's first line must state the business ("your surf-travel agency") immediately. It does (see `src/ui/dialogue.ts` once Task 8 lands).
- Guideline 5: no periods in card titles/headings; CTA buttons start with action verbs ("Plan this trip with the agent", "Confirm booking", "Book a session").
